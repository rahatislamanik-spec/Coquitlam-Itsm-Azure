The Situation

As Coquitlam College transitioned operations from Coquitlam to East Vancouver, the IT support workflow remained heavily dependent on manual processes.

Students from multiple programs and backgrounds may experience the same support model: send an email and wait. The project scenario explores how that process can be redesigned using cloud-native automation.

Operational Gaps

  • No structured intake process
  • No SLA tracking
  • No escalation workflow
  • No centralized visibility
  • No automation layer
  • No operational dashboard

Key Engineering Decisions

Decision Reason
Azure Service Bus instead of Storage QueueSupports enterprise messaging patterns and dead-letter queues.
Cosmos DB instead of traditional SQLFlexible NoSQL schema and scalable serverless design.
Managed Identity over hardcoded credentialsEliminates credential exposure.
Event-driven architectureSupports scalable independent workflows.
Azure FunctionsReduces infrastructure management overhead.
Application Insights + Log AnalyticsCentralized monitoring and observability.

Expected Outcome

The completed platform is intended to replace a reactive, human-dependent, email-based process with a scalable serverless system.

  • Accept requests 24/7 in a structured format
  • Route requests automatically by priority
  • Store records with audit trails and SLA deadlines
  • Notify students and staff automatically
  • Provide centralized monitoring and operational visibility
